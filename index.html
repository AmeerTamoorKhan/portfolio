About

Assistant Professor,
University of Copenhagen

I work at the intersection of AI, computer vision, plant phenotyping, precision agriculture, robotics, and edge-deployable intelligent systems — building robust pipelines that move from datasets and lab validation into real phenotyping facilities, field experiments, and resource-constrained hardware.

My research develops deployable AI for crop monitoring, plant growth prediction, disease localization, multi-view 3D plant reconstruction, and automated phenotyping workflows.

Current work at UCPH spans AI plant-image analysis, temporal growth modelling, high-throughput 3D reconstruction with 3–6 camera systems in the PhenoLab facility, and applied research in the IBIS project.
